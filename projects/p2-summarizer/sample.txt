Transformer models use a self-attention mechanism to process sequences in parallel,
making them far more efficient than the recurrent architectures they replaced.
They were first described in the 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need" by Vaswani et al.
The architecture relies on three core components: multi-head attention, position-wise
feed-forward layers, and positional encodings that inject sequence order information.

BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) pre-trains on masked
language modelling and is widely used for classification and question-answering tasks.
GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) uses a causal language model objective and
excels at text generation, code completion, and summarisation.

Teams adopting transformer-based models should audit their existing data pipelines for
tokenisation compatibility, retrain or fine-tune any legacy RNN-based classifiers, and
update their serving infrastructure to handle the higher memory footprint.
All documentation for retired RNN systems should be archived within the next quarter.
